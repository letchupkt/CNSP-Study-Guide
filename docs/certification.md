# CNSP Certification & Passing Criteria

## üìä Passing Score

The CNSP exam uses a straightforward scoring system:

| Score Range | Result | Description |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| **60-74%** | Pass | Successfully passed the exam |
| **75-100%** | Pass with Merit | Demonstrated higher level of competency |
| **0-59%** | Fail | Did not meet minimum passing requirements |

### What Does "Merit" Mean?

Achieving a **Pass with Merit** (75% or higher) indicates:
- Strong understanding of network security fundamentals
- Above-average competency in the tested domains
- Better preparation for advanced certifications
- Enhanced credibility with employers

## üìù How to Register & Take the Exam

### Step 1: Purchase Exam Voucher
1. Visit The SecOps Group website
2. Navigate to the CNSP certification page
3. Purchase an exam voucher
4. Receive voucher code via email

### Step 2: Prepare Your Testing Environment
Before scheduling, ensure you have:
- [ ] Laptop/desktop with webcam and microphone
- [ ] Stable internet connection (minimum 2 Mbps)
- [ ] Updated web browser (Chrome or Firefox recommended)
- [ ] Quiet, private testing space
- [ ] Valid government-issued ID
- [ ] Clear desk (remove all materials)

### Step 3: Schedule Your Exam
1. Log into your SecOps Group account
2. Enter your voucher code
3. Choose your exam date and time
4. The exam is **on-demand**, so you can take it immediately or schedule for later

### Step 4: Take the Exam
1. Log in at your scheduled time
2. Complete the AI proctoring setup:
   - Camera and microphone check
   - ID verification
   - Room scan
3. Review exam rules and instructions
4. Begin your 60-minute exam
5. Submit when complete

### Step 5: Receive Results
- Results are typically available **immediately** after submission
- AI proctoring logs are reviewed (usually quick)
- You'll see your score and pass/fail status

## ‚è∞ Certificate Validity

**Good News**: The CNSP certification **does not expire**!

**Important Notes**:
- Certificate is valid for life
- No renewal or recertification required
- Certificate shows the exam version you passed
- Industry standards evolve, so staying current is recommended
- Consider pursuing advanced certifications to demonstrate continued learning

**Exam Versions**:
- The SecOps Group may update exam content periodically
- Your certificate will indicate which version you passed
- Newer versions may cover updated technologies
- Holding an older version is still valid, but you may choose to retake for the latest version

## üéì After CNSP: Next Steps

### Recommended Progression Path

**Immediate Next Steps** (3-6 months):
- **CompTIA Security+**: Industry-standard security certification
- **CompTIA CySA+**: Cybersecurity Analyst certification

- **OSCP**: Offensive Security Certified Professional
- **CISSP**: Certified Information Systems Security Professional

### Continuing Education
- Stay updated with security news and trends
- Practice in CTF (Capture The Flag) competitions
- Contribute to security communities
- Build a home lab for continuous learning
- Attend security conferences (DEF CON, Black Hat, BSides)
